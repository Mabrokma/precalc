The following screenshots show how to solve the exponential equation `2^x=7` using your calculator.
Screenshots Keystrokes
1 Enter `y1` and `y2` as shown.
2 View your graph on the standard viewing window.
3 Press F5 and arrow down to "Intersection".
4 The cursor should go to `y1` by default; press ENTER.
5 The cursor should now go to `y2`; press ENTER.
6 The "Lower Bound" should be anywhere to the left of the point of intersection.
7 The "Upper Bound" should be anywhere to the right of the point of interesection.
8 The point of intersection should be shown.
Screenshots Keystrokes
1 Tap on the Graphs and Tables icon on the menu screen. Enter the two formulas shown into `y1` and `y2`. Make sure the items are checked and graph them.
2 Press the resize button at the bottom of the screen. Change your window if necessary to see both `y1` and `y2` clearly.
3 Once both graphs are clearly shown, tap on the "Analysis" menu at the top, then "G-solve" and select "Intersect".
4 The point of intersection automatically is found. The coordinates `(xc,yc)` . In this case, the intersection point is approximately at `(2.8073549,7)`. If you happened to have more than one intersection point, simply press the left or right arrows to scroll between the intersections.
